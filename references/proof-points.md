## Usage rules

1. **Always cite the segment context.** "~15% EPMV lift" is meaningless without "for sites with identified traffic." "110%+" is meaningless without "for U.S. identified users."
2. **Round to meaningful numbers.** "15%+" not "14.7%." "6x" not "613%." The story is the direction and magnitude, not false precision.
3. **Do not stack.** One primary "after" per section. Stacking multiple stats in a single paragraph dilutes — each one becomes background noise.
4. **Date-stamp in your head.** Performance numbers drift. Reusing Q1 stats in Q4 without re-validation is how we embarrass ourselves.
5. **Do not make CPM dollar claims** unless you have a cited source. "Our CPMs are the highest" is unverifiable and off-brand.

## Claims hygiene — before externalizing

1. **Segment context attached?** "15%+ EPMV lift" without "on identified traffic" is misleading.
2. **Directional vs. specific?** Use "~" or "up to" when the number is a range. Reserve specificity for audited, confirmed data.
3. **Still current?** Re-validate stats older than 6 months before putting them on a press release or landing page.
4. **No stacking.** One primary "after" per section.
5. **Publisher approval for named quotes.** Never externalize a customer quote without written approval.
6. **No unsourced CPM dollar claims.** Ever.
